Laplace Transform Definition (Integral Form)

$$\mathcal{L}\{f(t)\} = F(s) = \int_0^{\infty} f(t)\, e^{-st}\, dt$$

Key Pattern for These Problems

DC (Step): $f(t) = A \;\Rightarrow\; F(s) = \dfrac{A}{s}$    Table 2

Sine: $f(t) = A\sin(\omega t) \;\Rightarrow\; F(s) = \dfrac{A\omega}{s^2+\omega^2}$    Table 6

Ramp: $f(t) = At \;\Rightarrow\; F(s) = \dfrac{A}{s^2}$    Table 4

Decay exp: $f(t) = Ae^{-t/\tau} \;\Rightarrow\; F(s) = \dfrac{A\tau}{\tau s+1}$    Table 13a

Rising exp: $f(t) = A(1-e^{-t/\tau}) \;\Rightarrow\; F(s) = \dfrac{A}{s(\tau s+1)}$    Table 17a

Problem 1  — Sample values: 92 V DC · 89 Vp / 9 rad/s sine · 67 V/s ramp · 70 Vp / τ=4 s decay · 33 Vp / τ=6 s rise
1.1
What is the time domain equation of a 92 V DC voltage? (6.67 pts)
Answer
$$v(t) = 92 \text{ V}$$
Work
A DC voltage is constant over all time $t \geq 0$.
$$f(t) = A \quad\Rightarrow\quad v(t) = 92 \text{ V}$$
1.2
Using the integral definition, what is the Laplace domain equation of a 92 V DC voltage? (6.67 pts)
Answer
$$V(s) = \frac{92}{s}$$
Work — Integral Definition
$$\mathcal{L}\{92\} = \int_0^{\infty} 92\, e^{-st}\, dt$$
$$= 92 \int_0^{\infty} e^{-st}\, dt = 92 \left[\frac{e^{-st}}{-s}\right]_0^{\infty}$$
$$= 92 \left(0 - \frac{1}{-s}\right) = \frac{92}{s}$$
General form: $\mathcal{L}\{A\} = \dfrac{A}{s}$
1.3
What is the table line number for the Laplace Transform of a 92 V DC voltage? (6.67 pts)
Answer
Line 2
Work
Table entry 2: $F(s) = \dfrac{A}{s}$ corresponds to $f(t) = A$ (step/DC).   The A=92 constant is pulled out in front by the linearity property: $\mathcal{L}\{A \cdot f(t)\} = A \cdot \mathcal{L}\{f(t)\}$.
1.4
What is the time domain equation of a 89 Vp sine wave with ω = 9 rad/s and no phase shift? (6.67 pts)
Answer
$$v(t) = 89\sin(9t) \text{ V}$$
Work
General sine: $f(t) = A\sin(\omega t + \phi)$
$A = 89\text{ V}$, $\omega = 9\text{ rad/s}$, $\phi = 0$ (no phase shift)
$$v(t) = 89\sin(9t)$$
1.5
Using the integral definition, what is the Laplace of 89 Vp sine, ω = 9 rad/s? (6.67 pts)
Answer
$$V(s) = \frac{89 \cdot 9}{s^2 + 9^2} = \frac{801}{s^2 + 81}$$
Work — Integral Definition
$$\mathcal{L}\{89\sin(9t)\} = \int_0^{\infty} 89\sin(9t)\,e^{-st}\,dt$$
Pull constant out: $= 89 \int_0^{\infty} \sin(9t)\,e^{-st}\,dt$
Use known result $\int_0^\infty \sin(\omega t)e^{-st}dt = \dfrac{\omega}{s^2+\omega^2}$:
$$= 89 \cdot \frac{9}{s^2+81} = \frac{801}{s^2+81}$$
General form: $\mathcal{L}\{A\sin(\omega t)\} = \dfrac{A\omega}{s^2+\omega^2}$
1.6
What is the table line number for the Laplace of 89 Vp sine, ω = 9 rad/s? (6.67 pts)
Answer
Line 6
Work
Table entry 6: $F(s) = \dfrac{A\omega}{s^2+\omega^2}$ corresponds to $f(t) = A\sin(\omega t)$.
1.7
What is the time domain equation of a ramp starting at 0 V, rising 67 V in a second? (6.67 pts)
Answer
$$v(t) = 67t \text{ V}$$
Work
A ramp with slope $m$ V/s: $f(t) = mt$
Rises 67 V in 1 second → slope = 67 V/s
$$v(t) = 67t$$
1.8
Using the integral definition, what is the Laplace of a 67 V/s ramp? (6.67 pts)
Answer
$$V(s) = \frac{67}{s^2}$$
Work — Integral Definition
$$\mathcal{L}\{67t\} = \int_0^{\infty} 67t\,e^{-st}\,dt = 67\int_0^{\infty} t\,e^{-st}\,dt$$
Integrate by parts: $u = t$, $dv = e^{-st}dt$
$$= 67\left[\frac{-te^{-st}}{s}\Big|_0^\infty + \int_0^\infty \frac{e^{-st}}{s}\,dt\right] = 67\left[0 + \frac{1}{s^2}\right] = \frac{67}{s^2}$$
General form: $\mathcal{L}\{At\} = \dfrac{A}{s^2}$
1.9
What is the table line number for the Laplace of a 67 V/s ramp? (6.67 pts)
Answer
Line 4
Work
Table entry 4: $F(s) = \dfrac{A}{s^2}$ corresponds to $f(t) = At$ (ramp).
1.10
What is the time domain equation of a decaying exponential with peak 70 Vp and time constant τ = 4 s? (6.67 pts)
Answer
$$v(t) = 70\,e^{-t/4} \text{ V}$$
Work
Decaying exponential general form: $f(t) = A\,e^{-t/\tau}$
$A = 70\text{ V}$ (peak at $t=0$), $\tau = 4\text{ s}$
$$v(t) = 70\,e^{-t/4}$$
1.11
Using the integral definition, what is the Laplace of a 70 Vp decaying exp with τ = 4 s? (6.67 pts)
Answer
$$V(s) = \frac{70 \cdot 4}{4s+1} = \frac{280}{4s+1}$$
Work — Integral Definition
$$\mathcal{L}\{70\,e^{-t/4}\} = \int_0^{\infty} 70\,e^{-t/4}\,e^{-st}\,dt = 70\int_0^{\infty} e^{-(s+1/4)t}\,dt$$
$$= 70 \cdot \frac{1}{s+\frac{1}{4}} = \frac{70}{s+0.25} = \frac{70 \cdot 4}{4s+1} = \frac{280}{4s+1}$$
General form (τs form): $\mathcal{L}\left\{Ae^{-t/\tau}\right\} = \dfrac{A\tau}{\tau s+1}$
1.12
What is the table line number for the Laplace of a 70 Vp decaying exp, τ = 4 s? (Use τs form) (6.67 pts)
Answer
Line 13a
Work
Table entry 13a: $F(s) = \dfrac{A}{\tau s+1}$ corresponds to $f(t) = \dfrac{A}{\tau}e^{-t/\tau}$.
Our function $70e^{-t/4}$ matches with $A\tau = 70 \cdot 4 = 280$, so $F(s) = \dfrac{280}{4s+1}$. ✓
Note: The question says "Use the τs form" — that is entry 13a (not 13b which uses $s+a$ form).
1.13
What is the time domain equation of a rising exponential with peak 33 Vp and τ = 6 s? (6.67 pts)
Answer
$$v(t) = 33\!\left(1 - e^{-t/6}\right) \text{ V}$$
Work
Rising (charging) exponential: starts at 0, rises toward peak A.
General form: $f(t) = A(1 - e^{-t/\tau})$
$A = 33\text{ V}$, $\tau = 6\text{ s}$
$$v(t) = 33(1 - e^{-t/6})$$
Check: at $t=0$: $33(1-1) = 0$ ✓    as $t\to\infty$: $33(1-0) = 33$ V ✓
1.14
Using the integral definition, what is the Laplace of the 33 Vp rising exp, τ = 6 s? (6.67 pts)
Answer
$$V(s) = \frac{33}{s(6s+1)}$$
Work — Integral Definition
$$\mathcal{L}\{33(1-e^{-t/6})\} = 33\left[\mathcal{L}\{1\} - \mathcal{L}\{e^{-t/6}\}\right]$$
$$= 33\left[\frac{1}{s} - \frac{1}{s+\frac{1}{6}}\right] = 33\left[\frac{1}{s} - \frac{6}{6s+1}\right]$$
$$= 33 \cdot \frac{(6s+1) - 6s}{s(6s+1)} = 33 \cdot \frac{1}{s(6s+1)} = \frac{33}{s(6s+1)}$$
General form (τs form): $\mathcal{L}\{A(1-e^{-t/\tau})\} = \dfrac{A}{s(\tau s+1)}$
1.15
What is the table line number for the Laplace of 33 Vp rising exp, τ = 6 s? (Use τs form) (6.67 pts)
Answer
Line 17a
Work
Table entry 17a: $F(s) = \dfrac{A}{s(\tau s+1)}$ corresponds to $f(t) = A(1-e^{-t/\tau})$.
Note: "Use τs form" → entry 17a. (Entry 17b uses $s+a$ form.)
